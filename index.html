How do Big Ten universities differ in what fields they graduate students from? Use this tool to explore each institution’s degree-output profile across broad fields of study (CIP2), then compare institutions on any single field.

Quick start

  1. Select an institution to populate Figure 1 (treemap).
  2. Choose Major / Degree group / Award level to refine both charts.
  3. Select a CIP2 field (or click a treemap tile) to populate Figure 2 (comparison).
  4. Hover for counts and percentages; click a tile again to deselect.
Read more: definitions, how to read the charts, and counting rules

CIP stands for Classification of Instructional Programs, a taxonomy used by the U.S. Department of Education to categorize academic programs and fields of study across U.S. universities. Completion counts reported to IPEDS for each CIP2 category are used to show the distribution of graduates by field of study within each institution, as well as to compare the share of graduates in a selected field across all Big Ten institutions.

Figure 1 (Treemap) shows the composition of completions within a selected institution. Figure 2 (Comparison) shows the share of completions in a selected CIP2 field across all institutions under the same filters.

Counting note: students with multiple majors are counted in each relevant CIP2 category, consistent with how completions are reported by CIP.

Data & methodology

Data source

Data are based on completion counts reported to IPEDS for Academic Year 2023-2024, aggregated to the CIP2 level. Counts are based on IPEDS completions data aggregated at the CIP2 level and may differ slightly from institution-published figures due to reporting windows and classification standards.

How to read the visuals

  • Treemap: within the selected institution, each tile is a CIP2 area. The tile’s area is proportional to completions under the current filters.
  • Comparison chart: for the selected CIP2, each bar is: (CIP2 completions) ÷ (total completions) for that institution under the same non-institution filters.

Filters

  • Major: Major 1, Major 2, or All (combined).
  • Degree group: Category grouping for award types.
  • Award level: Award-level detail (or 'All'). 'All' is relative to the Degree group already selected.

Notes & limitations

  • Color is mapped deterministically by CIP2 code for consistency across sessions. This supports comparison but does not imply ranking or magnitude.
  • The comparison chart is intended for “share of completions” comparisons; it is not a count chart. Use hover tooltips for numerator/denominator values.
